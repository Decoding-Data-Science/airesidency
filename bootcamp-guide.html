The Project

DDS HR Enterprise Chatbot

Everyone in this bootcamp will build the same project — an HR Policy Chatbot that answers employee questions using real HR policy documents.

The Problem

Employees struggle to find HR policy information. Important details about leave, benefits, onboarding, and company rules are buried in long PDFs and internal portals.

Day 1 Checkpoint

What You Need to Submit

Complete this document filling guide for the HR Policy Chatbot. Submit after tonight's masterclass as text, Google Doc, PDF, or screenshot.

Important: Today is not about building the chatbot yet. Today is about understanding the problem clearly. A good AI application starts with clarity — who is the user, what problem are we solving, what data will we use, what should the chatbot do well?

Project Title

Use "HR Policy Chatbot" — you may also add a simple internal name.

Examples: HR Help Assistant · Employee Policy Bot · AskHR AI · HR Knowledge Assistant

Problem Statement

Write 2–4 sentences explaining the problem your chatbot solves.

Example: "Employees often struggle to find the right HR policy information quickly. Important details about leave, benefits, onboarding, and company rules are usually stored in long documents or internal portals. The HR Policy Chatbot will help employees ask questions in natural language and receive clear answers based on official HR documents."

Target User

Identify who will use the chatbot and how it helps them.

Example: "The chatbot is designed for employees who need quick answers about HR policies. It can also help HR teams reduce repetitive questions and improve access to policy information."

AI App Use Case

Write one clear sentence describing what the app will do.

Example: "The HR Policy Chatbot will allow employees to ask questions about HR policies and receive accurate, document-based answers with references where possible."

Input Data

Mention the type of HR content the chatbot will use as its knowledge source.

Sources: Employee handbook · Leave policy · WFH policy · Benefits policy · Code of conduct · Onboarding guide · Expense reimbursement policy · FAQ document · HR policy PDF

Sample User Questions

Add 5–10 questions that an employee may ask the chatbot.

• How many annual leave days do employees get? • What is the process to apply for sick leave? • Can I work from home? • What are the rules for maternity or paternity leave? • How do I claim business travel expenses? • What happens if I forget to mark attendance? • Where can I find the code of conduct? • What benefits are available to employees?

Expected Output

Describe what the chatbot should return when a user asks a question.

Example: "The chatbot should provide a clear answer using the HR policy document. The answer should be simple, professional, and easy to understand. Where possible, it should mention the relevant policy section or source document."

Definition of Done

Define what a successful first version looks like and your success metrics.

Example: "The first version will be successful if a user can ask HR policy questions and receive helpful answers based on the uploaded HR document. The chatbot should avoid giving answers not supported by the document and should clearly say when the information is not available."

Future Improvements (Optional)

Mention 2–3 improvements for later versions.

Ideas: Add source references · Support multiple HR documents · Employee-specific access control · Escalation to HR team · Multilingual support · Downloadable answer summaries · Feedback buttons for answer quality
